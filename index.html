Correlation Discovered Between State Incarceration Rates and Poverty

States with a higher percentage of poor tend to incarcerate more of their residents with southern states incarcerating the largest numbers on a per 100k basis. However, Delaware and Alaska, two non-poor states outside of the South incarcerate relatively large proportions of their population. The analysis makes no attempt whether poverty leads to incarceration or whether incarceration leads to poverty.
